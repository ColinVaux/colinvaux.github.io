City Lights

City nights
And city lights
And many lives to see

In timeless memories
Shaped by them
To shape then who they'll be

Their story's
Are their memories
And they keep making more

See their hunger
And such zest for life
Strikes me to my core

In some
I see resilience and
In some a lack of strife

An undergrad
holds their coffee,
Two hold hands, husband and wife

A couple
holding cardboard signs
Are just hoping for change

Handed a twenty
From a stock broker
Who just made an exchange

A woman talking
On the phone
To her sister living abroad

Evangelists
Hand pamphlets out
Telling all about their god

A man walks
With his boyfriend
Who tonight plans to propose

Though without
Their father's blessings
That's just sometimes how that goes

An officer
Patrols the block
Like clock work on the beat

So predictable
The local plug
Can alway be discreet

A waiter
Heading home
Stands while waiting for the bus

A parent
Picks their baby up
So it stops making a fuss

The bus driver
Keeps eyes ahead
Running from up to down

Then runs from down
Back up again
The bus cycles the town

And a poet
with a wandering eye
Went wandering tonight

What he saw
The little things in life
And in them he found delight


Night Drive

Rain on the roads
That smell in the air
city lights drove
Through the dark
With a glare

Wind rushing in
After hours of rush
Sparse in travelers
five lane road
Filled with a hush

Feeling a part
Of this feeling apart
We the stragglers
Staggering home
To restart

And the energy felt
From the storm up ahead
Giving promise of a
thunderous pour
Once in bed

And for just a moment
All that does consist
The journey
The promise
While we still exist


Mr. Stylistic

Dear Mr. Stylistic
What style will you pick today?

To say your sentiments
Like sediment
settles down
And erodes away

To say your drive
Is poison ivy
You despise
The dreadful itch

To say your lies
Just the oxygen
Mind sits within
Dark pitch

That your doubt
Is the ignition
Felt them flip
Ignitions switched

Then the flames
Of your self hatred
Burns each thread
Of thought thats stitched

But all thought
Is an illusion
Disillusion
Tempers regret

Your mind
Plagued by intrusions
To that end fear
Steers your set

Will you never let up
How you ever fret
what others view
of you will be

Mr. Stylistic,
I try to hide behind
you and the truth
I've broken
Sincerely,
Me


Midnight Oil

If there's beauty in not knowing
Does that beauty live unknown
Do the thoughts within me growing
Stem from someone else's poem

And Is that all that I am
a plot of land, the world to seed
Tending's hard not knowing,
Let what root and what to weed

And can this land, this soil bear
some fruit worthy to eat
Could life even survive here
Climate of bitter, cold and heat

impossible to pull from a fossils skull
The world which within did gleam
But those fossils make my soil full
within me oil flows in a stream

And so another midnight burn
As I churn some more out of me
to watch the dark of oil turn
to sternly claim the dark from night
and it sets me free
